# **📄 DOCUMENT 1**

## **Commercial Vision & Operating Principles**

### **Hala Logistics – Commercial App (Amin Al-Halabi)**

---

## **1\. Purpose of This Document**

This document defines the **commercial philosophy, operating principles, and decision logic** that the Hala Commercial App must enforce.

It is **not** a technical specification.  
It is the **commercial doctrine** that dictates:

* How decisions are made  
* How behavior is shaped  
* How data is interpreted  
* How complexity is controlled

All system design, workflows, dashboards, AI logic, and integrations must align with this document.

---

## **2\. Amin’s Core Commercial Reality**

### **2.1 The Problem Being Solved**

Hala does **not** suffer from a lack of data.  
It suffers from:

* Fragmented systems  
* Excel “farms”  
* Manual stitching of truth  
* Inflated pipelines  
* Delayed executive visibility

Critical commercial information exists, but it is:

* Spread across CRM, finance, operations, Tableau, and Excel  
* Updated manually  
* Trusted inconsistently  
* Interpreted too late

This creates **decision drag**, **false confidence**, and **reactive management**.

---

### **2.2 The Real Job of the Commercial Director**

Amin is not trying to:

* Micro-manage sales reps  
* Review raw CRM entries  
* Rebuild reports every month

Amin’s real job is to:

* Make **on-the-spot commercial decisions**  
* Allocate capacity intelligently  
* Decide **who to grow, who to fix, who to replace**  
* See reality early — not after billing

The Commercial App exists to **amplify executive judgment**, not replace it.

---

## **3\. The Commercial App Is Not a CRM**

### **3.1 Explicit Non-Goals**

The Commercial App is **not**:

* A CRM replacement (at least initially)  
* A sales activity tracker  
* A data entry system  
* A reporting toy

CRM remains operational.  
The Commercial App is **decisional**.

---

### **3.2 Noise vs Signal Doctrine**

Amin repeatedly emphasizes one rule:

**“I don’t want noise. I want signal.”**

This means:

* Early CRM stages (prospecting, early leads) are **noise**  
* Executive attention starts at **qualified opportunity**  
* Visibility is earned by relevance, not volume

The Commercial App must:

* Filter aggressively  
* Surface only what requires **executive action**  
* Hide everything else by default

---

## **4\. Single Source of Commercial Truth**

### **4.1 Canonical Truth Principle**

The Commercial App becomes the **single source of commercial truth**, even if data originates elsewhere.

Other systems may:

* Generate data  
* Store data  
* Process transactions

But **commercial truth is decided here**.

This eliminates:

* Conflicting reports  
* Multiple versions of reality  
* “Which number is right?” conversations

---

### **4.2 Trust Hierarchy**

When numbers differ:

1. Commercial App truth wins  
2. Source systems are investigated  
3. Root cause is fixed, not worked around

This forces **discipline across the ecosystem**.

---

## **5\. Decision-First System Design**

### **5.1 Decisions Before Dashboards**

Dashboards are **not the product**.  
Decisions are.

Every screen, metric, and signal must answer:

* *What decision does this enable?*  
* *Who must act?*  
* *By when?*

If no decision exists, the data does not belong in the Commercial App.

---

### **5.2 Red / Amber / Green Over Numbers**

Executives do not need spreadsheets.

They need:

* Risk signals  
* Bottlenecks  
* Delays  
* Exceptions

Numbers exist **behind** signals, not instead of them.

---

## **6\. Behavior Is Shaped by Systems**

### **6.1 The Mindset Control Principle**

Amin’s long-term goal is explicit:

Control behavior by controlling **process, system, and language**.

This means:

* Sales teams behave according to what the system enforces  
* Updates happen because the system demands them  
* Discipline becomes automatic, not enforced manually

The Commercial App is a **behavior-shaping mechanism**.

---

### **6.2 Manual Chasing Is Failure**

If Amin has to:

* Chase go-live dates  
* Ask for pipeline updates  
* Manually reconcile reports

The system has already failed.

The app must:

* Detect staleness  
* Flag risk  
* Escalate automatically

---

## **7\. Reality-Based Commercial Forecasting**

### **7.1 Pipeline ≠ Revenue**

A large pipeline means nothing if:

* Probabilities are inflated  
* Go-live dates slip silently  
* Billing reality diverges from promises

The Commercial App must distinguish between:

* **Optimism**  
* **Expectation**  
* **Reality**

---

### **7.2 Probability-Weighted Truth**

Commercial forecasting must be:

* Probability-weighted  
* Time-aware  
* Continuously updated

A billion-riyal pipeline at 5% probability is not success.  
It is risk disguised as confidence.

---

## **8\. Customer Quality Over Customer Quantity**

### **8.1 The Customer Classification Doctrine**

Not all revenue is good revenue.

Customers must be evaluated on:

* Commercial value  
* Operational complexity  
* Financial behavior

A low-grade customer consuming high capacity is a **strategic threat**, not a win.

---

### **8.2 Replace, Don’t Tolerate**

The Commercial App must help answer:

* Should we renew this customer?  
* Should we fix this customer?  
* Should we replace this customer with a better ICP?

This shifts the mindset from **retention at all costs** to **portfolio optimization**.

---

## **9\. Baseline, Versioning & Accountability**

### **9.1 Baseline Is Sacred**

Once a deal is:

* Approved  
* Signed  
* Baselined

It becomes a **reference truth**.

Changes are:

* Versioned  
* Audited  
* Explained

No silent drift.  
No memory-based negotiations.

---

### **9.2 Commercial Memory**

The system must remember:

* What was agreed  
* When it changed  
* Why it changed  
* Who approved it

This protects both:

* Executive authority  
* Organizational integrity

---

## **10\. AI as Advisor, Not Decision-Maker**

### **10.1 AI in the Loop, Not in Control**

AI exists to:

* Accelerate proposals  
* Analyze risk  
* Highlight anomalies

AI does **not**:

* Approve deals  
* Override judgment  
* Replace accountability

Human agency is non-negotiable.

---

## **11\. MVP Philosophy**

### **11.1 Build the Spine First**

The first release must:

* Create immediate executive value  
* Lock the structural foundations  
* Avoid premature sophistication

A strong base allows:

* Faster iteration  
* Safer automation  
* Scalable intelligence

---

## **12\. Final Principle: Commercial Orchestration**

The end vision is **digital commercial orchestration**:

* Data flows automatically  
* Signals emerge early  
* Decisions are faster  
* Capacity is optimized  
* Revenue quality improves

The Commercial App is the **conductor**, not the orchestra.

---

### **✅ Outcome of Document 1**

After this document:

* Architects know *what must never be violated*  
* Developers understand *why features exist*  
* Executives recognize *their thinking in the system*  
* Scope debates become simpler and faster

---

# **📄 DOCUMENT 2**

## **Commercial App – Functional Requirements**

### **Hala Logistics (Commercial Director: Amin)**

---

## **1\. Purpose of This Document**

This document defines the **functional capabilities** the Commercial App must provide.

It translates:

* Commercial intent → system behavior  
* Executive needs → app functions  
* Process chaos → enforced structure

This is the **authoritative functional scope** for:

* MVP definition  
* Architecture design  
* Feature prioritization  
* Acceptance criteria

---

## **2\. User Roles & Authority Levels**

### **2.1 Core User Roles**

The system must support, at minimum, the following roles:

| Role | Description |
| ----- | ----- |
| Commercial Director | Executive decision-maker (Amin) |
| Commercial Manager | Deal owner, proposal owner |
| Sales | CRM-originating role (mostly upstream) |
| Finance | Billing, profitability, DSO inputs |
| Operations | Service complexity & capacity inputs |
| Administrator | System configuration & governance |

---

### **2.2 Authority Enforcement**

The system must:

* Enforce **role-based permissions**  
* Respect the **authority matrix** provided by Amin  
* Control who can:  
  * Create documents  
  * Edit documents  
  * Approve documents  
  * Unlock baselines

Authority is **system-enforced**, not advisory.

---

## **3\. Commercial Workspace (Core Object)**

### **3.1 Definition**

A **Commercial Workspace** represents **one client / one commercial context**.

Everything related to that client lives inside the workspace.

---

### **3.2 Workspace Contents**

Each workspace must contain:

* Client identity & metadata  
* Deal type (new / renewal / tender / quote)  
* CRM-linked opportunities  
* Proposals  
* Quotes  
* SLAs  
* Approvals  
* Economics  
* Version history  
* Audit trail

---

### **3.3 Workspace Lifecycle**

A workspace must move through defined states:

1. Created (auto-triggered from CRM)  
2. Active  
3. In Review  
4. Approved  
5. Baselined  
6. Renewed / Amended  
7. Closed / Discontinued

State changes must be **event-driven** and **logged**.

---

## **4\. Dashboard Requirements**

### **4.1 Executive Dashboard (Primary)**

The Executive Dashboard is **the default landing view** for Amin.

It must show:

* Active workspaces count  
* Deals requiring approval  
* Stalled items  
* Pipeline value (probability-weighted)  
* Go-live risks  
* SLA or proposal delays

All elements must answer:

*“What needs my attention now?”*

---

### **4.2 Signal-Based Indicators**

The dashboard must use:

* Red / Amber / Green indicators  
* Time-based escalation flags  
* Priority labels (Urgent / Important)

Raw tables are **not acceptable** for executive views.

---

## **5\. CRM Integration & Pipeline Synchronisation**

### **5.1 CRM Relationship**

The Commercial App must:

* Integrate with the existing CRM (Zoho initially)  
* **Not duplicate CRM data entry**  
* Treat CRM as the *source of opportunities*, not decisions

---

### **5.2 Pipeline Mirroring Rules**

CRM pipeline stages must be:

* Mirrored visually inside the Commercial App  
* Read-only for early stages (Prospecting, Lead)  
* Actionable only once reaching **Qualified**

This enforces **noise filtering**.

---

### **5.3 Trigger Events**

At minimum, the following CRM events must trigger Commercial App actions:

| CRM Event | Commercial App Action |
| ----- | ----- |
| Opportunity reaches Qualified | Create workspace |
| Stage moves to Proposal | Signal Amin |
| Proposal Sent | Lock draft |
| Contract Signed | Baseline |
| Go-live date passes | Risk alert |

---

## **6\. Proposal, Quote & SLA Management**

### **6.1 Document Types**

The system must support:

* Commercial Proposals  
* Pricing Quotes  
* SLAs (Service Level Agreements)  
* Contract references (read-only initially)

Each document type must:

* Have a lifecycle  
* Support versioning  
* Be auditable

---

### **6.2 AI-Assisted Creation**

Users must be able to:

* Upload meeting transcripts  
* Upload voice notes  
* Provide structured prompts

AI must:

* Generate draft content  
* Structure documents according to Hala standards  
* Highlight risks (pricing, complexity, margin)

AI output must **never auto-approve**.

---

### **6.3 Editor with Human Agency**

The editor must:

* Allow block-based editing  
* Allow reordering and deletion  
* Track human vs AI-generated content  
* Enforce authority rules on edits

---

## **7\. Approval & Escalation Workflow**

### **7.1 Approval Gates**

Documents must pass through defined gates:

1. Draft  
2. Review  
3. Approval  
4. Baselined

Skipping gates must:

* Require authority  
* Be logged  
* Be visible in audit trails

---

### **7.2 Escalation Rules**

If a document:

* Remains idle too long  
* Awaits approval beyond SLA  
* Misses a go-live milestone

The system must:

* Escalate automatically  
* Notify responsible roles  
* Flag Amin when thresholds are breached

---

## **8\. Economics & Baseline Control**

### **8.1 Baseline Definition**

A baseline represents:

* Final approved commercial truth  
* Locked pricing & SLA terms  
* Reference point for renewals

---

### **8.2 Versioning**

The system must:

* Preserve previous versions  
* Support renewal-based cloning  
* Track delta changes explicitly

Silent overwrites are **not allowed**.

---

## **9\. Customer Classification (ECR)**

### **9.1 ECR Purpose**

The Existing Customer Rating (ECR) must:

* Support renewal decisions  
* Highlight misaligned customers  
* Protect capacity strategy

---

### **9.2 ECR Criteria (Phase 1\)**

Phase 1 must support **manual scoring** across:

* Commercial value  
* Operational complexity  
* Financial behavior

Output:

* Grade (A–F)  
* Renewal recommendation

---

### **9.3 ECR Automation (Phase 2\)**

Later phases may automate scoring using:

* Finance data  
* Operations data  
* CRM history

---

## **10\. Finance & Billing Visibility**

### **10.1 Required Finance Inputs**

The system must support integration for:

* Actual billing  
* Revenue realization  
* DSO  
* Profitability

---

### **10.2 Reality vs Promise**

The app must highlight gaps between:

* Expected revenue  
* Actual billing  
* Delayed go-live impacts

This is critical for **forecast credibility**.

---

## **11\. Tableau Strategy (Functional Requirement)**

### **11.1 Phase 1**

* Support embedded Tableau views  
* Executive-only access  
* Read-only

---

### **11.2 Phase 2+**

* Gradual replacement with native dashboards  
* Reuse validated metrics  
* Eliminate duplicate reporting

---

## **12\. Audit Trail & Governance**

The system must record:

* Who changed what  
* When it changed  
* Why it changed  
* Under which authority

Audit logs must be:

* Immutable  
* Searchable  
* Executive-accessible

---

## **13\. MVP Functional Scope (Non-Negotiable)**

MVP **must include**:

* Commercial Workspaces  
* Executive Dashboard  
* CRM Qualified-stage sync  
* Proposal generation  
* Approval workflow  
* Baseline locking  
* Manual ECR

Anything beyond this is **Phase 2+**.

---

## **14\. Explicit Non-Functional Boundaries**

The Commercial App must **not**:

* Become a CRM clone  
* Allow uncontrolled edits  
* Require Amin to chase updates  
* Rely on Excel for truth

---

## **15\. Outcome of Document 2**

After this document:

* Product knows **what to build**  
* Architecture knows **what to support**  
* Developers know **what “done” means**  
* Scope creep becomes visible immediately

# **📄 DOCUMENT 3**

## **Dashboards, Metrics & Decision Signals**

### **Hala Logistics – Commercial App**

---

## **1\. Purpose of This Document**

This document defines:

* What dashboards exist  
* What metrics are allowed  
* How signals are generated  
* How executive attention is directed

It explicitly rejects:

* Dashboard clutter  
* Vanity metrics  
* Analyst-style reporting inside the Commercial App

The Commercial App is a **decision cockpit**, not a BI tool.

---

## **2\. Dashboard Philosophy**

### **2.1 Signal Over Data**

Dashboards exist to answer **one question**:

*“What decision must I make now?”*

If a metric does not:

* Trigger action  
* Change behavior  
* Influence a decision

It does **not belong** in an executive dashboard.

---

### **2.2 Executive Attention Is Scarce**

Amin must never:

* Scroll endlessly  
* Interpret raw tables  
* Cross-reference systems mentally

Dashboards must:

* Compress reality  
* Highlight exceptions  
* Surface risk early

---

## **3\. Dashboard Hierarchy**

The Commercial App must implement **three dashboard layers**:

1. Executive Dashboard (Primary)  
2. Workspace-Level Dashboards  
3. Embedded Analytical Views (Secondary / Optional)

Only **Layer 1 and 2** are core to MVP.

---

## **4\. Executive Dashboard (Primary View)**

### **4.1 Role**

This is the **default landing screen** for Amin.

It provides:

* Instant situational awareness  
* Actionable alerts  
* Priority focus areas

---

### **4.2 Mandatory Sections**

The Executive Dashboard must include:

#### **A. Attention Required Panel**

Shows:

* Deals awaiting approval  
* Breached SLAs  
* Stalled proposals  
* Go-live delays

This panel is **non-dismissible**.

---

#### **B. Pipeline Reality Panel**

Shows:

* Total pipeline (probability-weighted)  
* Pipeline by stage  
* Pipeline by expected go-live window

Explicitly **excludes**:

* Raw CRM pipeline totals  
* Inflated opportunity values

---

#### **C. Risk Signals Panel**

Shows:

* Under-quoting risk  
* Capacity risk  
* Customer quality risk  
* Renewal risk

Displayed as **Red / Amber / Green** only.

---

#### **D. Time Sensitivity Panel**

Shows:

* Items nearing SLA breach  
* Approval ageing  
* Deals stuck without movement

Time is treated as a **risk multiplier**.

---

## **5\. Signal Types (Core Concept)**

Signals are **system-generated interpretations**, not user-entered data.

### **5.1 Signal Categories**

| Category | Purpose |
| ----- | ----- |
| Attention Signals | Require executive action |
| Risk Signals | Highlight potential loss |
| Opportunity Signals | Highlight upside |
| Compliance Signals | Show process violations |

---

### **5.2 Signal Characteristics**

Every signal must include:

* Signal type  
* Severity (Red / Amber / Green)  
* Trigger reason  
* Time since trigger  
* Recommended action (textual)

Signals must be **clickable**, opening context — never raw data.

---

## **6\. Red / Amber / Green Logic**

### **6.1 Red (Immediate Attention)**

Triggered when:

* Approval SLA breached  
* Go-live date passed without update  
* High-risk customer consuming critical capacity  
* Proposal stalled beyond defined threshold

Red signals **escalate automatically**.

---

### **6.2 Amber (Monitor Closely)**

Triggered when:

* Probability drops  
* Dates approach without confirmation  
* Customer quality deteriorates  
* Financial signals trend negatively

Amber signals **do not escalate**, but remain visible.

---

### **6.3 Green (Healthy)**

Indicates:

* Movement is on track  
* No immediate risk  
* No executive action required

Green signals **fade into background**.

---

## **7\. Workspace-Level Dashboards**

Each Commercial Workspace must have its own dashboard view.

### **7.1 Workspace Dashboard Purpose**

To answer:

* Where is this deal now?  
* What is blocking it?  
* What decisions are pending?  
* What risk exists?

---

### **7.2 Workspace Dashboard Components**

Each workspace must show:

* Current stage  
* Document status (proposal, SLA, quote)  
* Approval state  
* Time in stage  
* Risk indicators  
* ECR grade (if existing customer)

This view is **contextual**, not comparative.

---

## **8\. Metrics That Are Allowed**

The Commercial App only allows **decision-grade metrics**.

### **8.1 Pipeline Metrics**

Allowed:

* Probability-weighted value  
* Stage distribution  
* Go-live–based forecast

Not allowed:

* Raw opportunity count  
* Vanity pipeline inflation

---

### **8.2 Time Metrics**

Allowed:

* Time in stage  
* Time since last update  
* Time to approval

Time is always treated as **risk**, not performance vanity.

---

### **8.3 Customer Metrics**

Allowed:

* Customer grade (ECR)  
* Capacity utilization vs grade  
* Renewal risk

Not allowed:

* Revenue-only customer ranking

---

## **9\. Metrics Explicitly Excluded**

The following **must not appear** in executive dashboards:

* Activity counts (calls, meetings)  
* CRM lead volume  
* Sales rep performance stats  
* Raw Excel-derived tables

These belong in **operational tools**, not the Commercial App.

---

## **10\. Go-Live & Revenue Reality Dashboard**

### **10.1 Purpose**

To expose the gap between:

* Expected revenue  
* Promised go-live  
* Actual billing

---

### **10.2 Required Indicators**

* Upcoming go-lives (next 30/60/90 days)  
* Missed go-lives  
* Revenue at risk due to delay  
* Confirmed billing vs forecast

This directly supports **financial credibility**.

---

## **11\. Customer Portfolio View (Executive)**

### **11.1 Portfolio Logic**

This is **not** a customer list.

It is a **portfolio health view**, showing:

* High-quality customers  
* Low-quality, high-consumption customers  
* Replaceable revenue

---

### **11.2 Strategic Questions Answered**

* Which customers should we renew?  
* Which customers should we fix?  
* Which customers should we exit?

This view supports **capacity strategy**, not sales activity.

---

## **12\. Tableau & External Analytics**

### **12.1 Phase 1 Rule**

If Tableau exists:

* Embed selectively  
* Executive-only  
* Read-only

Tableau **must not** drive decisions independently of the Commercial App.

---

### **12.2 Phase 2 Rule**

Validated metrics may be:

* Rebuilt natively  
* Simplified  
* Turned into signals

The Commercial App always remains the **decision authority**.

---

## **13\. Alerting & Escalation**

### **13.1 Alert Channels**

Alerts must be delivered via:

* In-app notifications  
* Email (selective)  
* Future: messaging tools

---

### **13.2 Escalation Rules**

Alerts escalate when:

* Ignored  
* Time thresholds breached  
* Risk severity increases

Escalation paths are **role-based**.

---

## **14\. Anti-Patterns to Avoid**

The system must not:

* Allow dashboard sprawl  
* Add metrics without decision mapping  
* Expose raw CRM dumps  
* Reward pipeline inflation

---

## **15\. Outcome of Document 3**

After this document:

* Dashboards are no longer subjective  
* “What should I look at?” disappears  
* Decisions surface themselves  
* Executive time is protected

.

# **📄 DOCUMENT 4**

## **Data Sources, Integration & APIs**

### **Hala Logistics – Commercial App**

---

## **1\. Purpose of This Document**

This document defines:

* All upstream and downstream data sources  
* Ownership of data by domain  
* Synchronisation rules  
* API responsibilities  
* What is **read**, **written**, or **mirrored**

It prevents:

* Data duplication  
* Conflicting truths  
* Integration chaos  
* “Let’s just pull everything” mistakes

---

## **2\. Core Integration Philosophy**

### **2.1 Canonical Commercial Layer**

The Commercial App is the **canonical commercial decision layer**.

This means:

* It does **not** replace systems immediately  
* It **orchestrates** them  
* It consumes data, applies logic, and produces decisions

Source systems remain **system-of-record** for their domain — but **not system-of-decision**.

---

### **2.2 Read \> Interpret \> Signal**

All integrations follow the same pattern:

1. **Read** data from source systems  
2. **Interpret** data inside the Commercial App  
3. **Generate signals & decisions**  
4. **Optionally write back outcomes**

No system bypasses this chain.

---

## **3\. System Landscape Overview**

| System | Role |
| ----- | ----- |
| CRM (Zoho initially) | Opportunity & lead system of record |
| Finance System | Billing, DSO, profitability |
| Operations / WMS / D365 | Capacity, utilization, service complexity |
| Tableau | Analytical views (temporary) |
| Excel (legacy) | Transitional inputs only |
| Commercial App | Canonical decision layer |

---

## **4\. CRM Integration (Zoho – Phase 1\)**

### **4.1 CRM Ownership**

CRM owns:

* Leads  
* Opportunities  
* Sales pipeline stages  
* Sales inputs

CRM does **not** own:

* Commercial approval  
* Pricing authority  
* Deal baselines  
* Executive signals

---

### **4.2 Data Read from CRM**

The Commercial App must read:

* Opportunity ID  
* Client ID  
* Stage  
* Probability  
* Expected go-live date  
* Expected volume  
* Expected revenue  
* Sales owner  
* Last updated timestamp

This data is **read-only** inside the Commercial App.

---

### **4.3 CRM → Commercial Triggers**

| CRM Event | Commercial App Action |
| ----- | ----- |
| Opportunity reaches “Qualified” | Create Commercial Workspace |
| Stage changes | Recalculate signals |
| Go-live date updated | Update forecast |
| Opportunity closed won | Initiate baseline |
| Opportunity stalled | Generate risk signal |

---

### **4.4 Write-Back to CRM (Controlled)**

The Commercial App may write back:

* Proposal sent status  
* Approval milestones  
* Baseline confirmation  
* Go-live confirmation

Write-back is:

* Event-based  
* Explicit  
* Logged

---

## **5\. Finance Integration**

### **5.1 Finance Ownership**

Finance owns:

* Actual billing  
* Revenue realization  
* DSO  
* Profitability

Commercial App **consumes**, not overrides finance truth.

---

### **5.2 Data Read from Finance**

Required inputs:

* Monthly billing per customer  
* Actual revenue vs forecast  
* DSO status  
* Margin / profitability indicators

Initial phase may use:

* Monthly batch uploads  
* Structured exports

Phase 2+ moves to:

* API or data pipeline integration

---

### **5.3 Finance Signals Generated**

The Commercial App must generate:

* Revenue gap alerts  
* Delayed billing risk  
* Customer profitability degradation  
* Renewal risk based on financial behavior

Finance never generates these signals directly.

---

## **6\. Operations / D365 / WMS Integration**

### **6.1 Operations Ownership**

Operations owns:

* Capacity  
* Utilization  
* Service complexity  
* Warehouse constraints

---

### **6.2 Data Read from Operations**

The Commercial App must ingest:

* Capacity per facility  
* Utilization %  
* Volume per customer  
* Complexity indicators (manual, special handling, etc.)

This data feeds:

* ECR scoring  
* Capacity risk signals  
* Deal feasibility analysis

---

### **6.3 Operational Risk Signals**

Examples:

* Low-grade customer consuming high capacity  
* High-complexity customer with low margin  
* Over-commitment risk

These signals are **executive-facing**, not operational KPIs.

---

## **7\. Customer Classification (ECR) Data Flow**

### **7.1 Phase 1 (Manual)**

Inputs:

* Commercial assessment (manual)  
* Operational assessment (manual)  
* Financial assessment (manual)

Stored inside the Commercial App.

---

### **7.2 Phase 2 (Automated)**

Automated inputs from:

* Finance (DSO, profitability)  
* Operations (complexity, utilization)  
* CRM (tenure, growth potential)

Commercial App calculates:

* ECR score  
* ECR grade  
* Renewal recommendation

---

## **8\. Proposal, Quote & SLA Data**

### **8.1 Internal Ownership**

Proposals, quotes, and SLAs are **owned by the Commercial App**.

They are:

* Created here  
* Approved here  
* Baselined here

CRM receives **status**, not content.

---

### **8.2 AI Inputs**

AI systems ingest:

* Uploaded transcripts  
* Voice notes
